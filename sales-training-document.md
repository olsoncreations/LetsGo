## PART 1: KNOW THE PRODUCT

### What Is LetsGo?

LetsGo is a discovery and rewards platform that connects local businesses with customers who get paid to come back. Users find restaurants, bars, activities, salons, and entertainment venues through our app, visit them in person, upload their receipt, and earn progressive cash-back rewards that increase the more they visit the same business.

**Tagline**: Go. Play. Eat. Get paid to live your best life.

**The core loop**: Discover → Visit → Upload Receipt → Earn Cash Back → Come Back Again

Unlike traditional advertising where businesses pay for impressions, clicks, or coupons that may never convert, LetsGo businesses only pay when a real, verified customer walks through the door and spends real money. Every dollar a business pays through LetsGo is tied directly to a confirmed sale.

### How It Works — The 30-Second Pitch

"LetsGo puts your business in front of local customers who are actively looking for places to go. When they visit you and spend money, they upload their receipt and earn cash back — starting at 5% and increasing up to 20% the more they come back. You only pay when a real customer makes a real purchase. No monthly fees on our Basic plan, no paying for clicks or views, and every penny you spend is tied to verified revenue walking through your door."

### Why LetsGo Is Different From Everything Else

**vs. Yelp / Google Ads**: Those platforms charge for clicks and impressions. You pay whether someone visits or not. LetsGo charges only on verified receipts — real customers, real spending.

**vs. Groupon / Deal Sites**: Groupon attracts deal-seekers who come once for a discount and never return. LetsGo's progressive model does the opposite — it rewards repeat visits, turning first-timers into regulars. The cash-back percentage goes UP the more someone visits.

**vs. Loyalty Cards / Punch Cards**: Punch cards get lost. They don't attract new customers. They don't give you data. LetsGo handles discovery, loyalty, and analytics in one platform, and the customer never has to carry anything extra.

**vs. Social Media Advertising**: Social media ads require creative content, ongoing management, and uncertain ROI. LetsGo requires no content creation from the business and ties every dollar directly to a verified sale.

**The Bottom Line**: LetsGo is the only platform where businesses pay exclusively for results — verified customer visits with proven spending — while building a loyalty engine that makes those customers come back more and more often.

## PART 3: THE PROGRESSIVE PAYOUT SYSTEM

This is the heart of LetsGo and the most important thing for salespeople to understand and communicate clearly.

### How Progressive Payouts Work

Every business on LetsGo sets up a 7-tier payout structure. The cash-back percentage a customer earns increases with every visit they make to that specific business. This creates a powerful incentive loop: the more someone visits, the more they earn per visit, which motivates them to keep coming back.

**Key details**:
- Each user-business relationship is tracked independently
- Visit counts are based on a 365-day rolling window (resets annually)
- Only approved receipts count toward visit totals
- The payout comes from the receipt subtotal (before tax and tip)
- There is no per-receipt cap on the payout amount

## PART 5: WHAT USERS EXPERIENCE (Know What You're Selling)

Understanding the user side of the platform helps you sell to businesses. When a business owner asks "What do my customers actually see?", you need clear answers.

### 6 Ways Users Discover Businesses

**1. Discovery Feed (Explore New Adventures)**
The main experience. Users swipe through full-screen business cards like a dating app for places to go. Each business gets three panels: a hero photo, a details page (with address, hours, payout tiers, and description), and a photo gallery. Users can filter by distance, price level, cuisine, vibe, dietary needs, amenities, and more. They can sort by nearest, most popular, highest payout, trending, and other options. Every business card shows all 7 payout tiers upfront so users can see exactly how much they'll earn.

**2. Date Night Generator (Premium businesses only)**
For couples who can't decide where to go. The app picks one restaurant and one activity automatically. The user either accepts ("Lock In") or asks for another suggestion. This drives spontaneous visits — users didn't plan to go to your restaurant, but the app recommended it and they went.

**3. 5 → 3 → 1 Pick (Two-person game)**
Two friends each pick 5 places they'd be interested in visiting. They vote together to narrow it down to 3, then vote again to pick 1 winner. This turns "where should we go?" into a fun, interactive game. Your business could be the one that wins the vote.

**4. Group Vote (Friend groups)**
Same concept as 5v3v1 but for larger groups. Everyone adds options and votes. The group narrows it down democratically. Perfect for friend groups, coworkers, or family outings. Games include mini-games in the waiting room to keep people engaged while everyone joins.

**5. Events Feed (Premium businesses only)**
Users browse upcoming events — live music, trivia nights, tastings, comedy shows, workshops, and more. Businesses create events with details, pricing, and capacity. Users can RSVP and see how many others are going. This drives planned visits for specific occasions.

**6. Experiences Feed (Premium businesses only)**
A social feed of real photos and videos uploaded by customers who visited local businesses. Users browse user-generated content to see what places actually look like and what the vibe is. Businesses moderate what gets posted. This is social proof that drives visits.

### The Receipt Upload Process

After visiting a business, the user:
1. Opens their Profile in the app
2. Taps "Add Receipt"
3. Searches for the business
4. Uploads a photo of their receipt
5. Enters the subtotal amount and visit date
6. Submits the receipt

The receipt shows as "Pending" until the business approves it. Once approved, the cash-back is added to the user's balance. Users get a push notification when their receipt is approved or rejected.

### User Earnings & Cashout

- Users see their lifetime earnings, this month's earnings, pending payouts, and available balance on their Profile
- Minimum cashout is $20.00
- Payout methods: Venmo, PayPal, or bank transfer (ACH)
- Processing time: 3 business days after request

### The Loyalty Loop

Users see a progress bar for each business showing their current tier and how many more visits they need to level up. When they reach a new tier, they get a celebration notification. This gamification drives repeat visits — users want to "level up" at their favorite spots, which means more revenue for the business.

After a business signs up, they get a full management dashboard. Knowing what's in the dashboard helps you sell the value of the platform and reassure business owners that they have full control.

### 9 Dashboard Tabs

**1. Overview**: At-a-glance KPIs — pending receipts count and dollar value, upcoming events, active ad campaigns, media count, latest invoice total. Quick links to every other section.

**8. Profile Settings**: Edit all public-facing business information — name, description, type, cuisine, price level, hours, location, phone, website, age restrictions, tags, and logo. Publish changes to make the business discoverable in the feed.

**9. Support & Help**: In-app messaging with the LetsGo support team. File attachments for screenshots and documents. Searchable knowledge base with articles on getting started, receipt management, events, advertising, billing, and troubleshooting. Message history and status tracking.

## PART 7: THE ONBOARDING PROCESS

When you close a sale, the business owner goes through a 7-step online signup that takes about 15 minutes total. Knowing this flow lets you walk them through it or answer questions.

### Step 1: Business Basics (~4 minutes)
Business name, business type (restaurant/bar, activity, salon/beauty, retail, event venue, other), contact person's name, email, phone, and role. Referral field to track which salesperson brought them in.

### Step 2: Location & Hours (~3 minutes)
Street address with Google Maps autocomplete (auto-fills city, state, ZIP). Business phone, customer-facing email, website. Public business name (what customers see — can differ from legal name). Operating hours for each day of the week. Age restriction setting (all ages, 18+, or 21+).

### Step 3: Plan Selection (~2.5 minutes)
Choose Basic (free) or Premium ($100/month). Select any premium add-ons. Optionally add advertising campaigns. Option to request a custom advertising consultation.

### Step 4: Payout Configuration (~2 minutes)
Choose a preset (Conservative, Standard, Aggressive) or build custom tiers. Review the 7-tier structure. Set the receipt verification contact. Optionally enable auto-approval with a dollar threshold.

### Step 5: Payment Setup (~1.5 minutes)
Secure payment setup through Stripe. Choose bank account (no processing fee) or credit/debit card (3.5% fee). Billing address. Review authorization for charges (payouts, platform fees, subscription, advertising, processing fees).

### Step 6: Verification & Logo (~1 minute)
Upload one verification document — business license, EIN certificate, or utility bill matching the business name and address. Upload business logo (required, square format, 500x500px minimum).

### Step 7: Review & Sign
Review all marketing permissions (allow user uploads, feature in discovery, participate in testing). Review optional services (promotional video help, profile management). Accept four legal agreements (Terms of Service, Billing Policy, Content Policy, Fraud Liability). Digital signature. Review total cost summary. Submit.

### After Submission

The business sees a success screen: "Welcome to LetsGo! Your business profile has been submitted for review."

What happens next:
- Within 24 hours: LetsGo verifies documents and activates the account
- Business receives an email notification when approved
- Once approved, the business appears in the Discovery feed and can start receiving customers

### "We already have enough customers."

"That's great — and LetsGo isn't just about getting new customers. It's about making sure the customers you already have keep coming back. Our progressive payout system rewards repeat visits, which means higher lifetime value per customer. And with our Discovery feed, you'll also reach new customers who are actively looking for a place to go tonight. More customers plus more repeat visits equals more revenue."

### "We can't afford to pay customers cash back."

"I understand the concern, but let's look at the numbers. On the Standard plan, a first-time customer spending $40 gets $2 back. That's 5% — less than most credit card processing fees. And you're only paying that when a real customer makes a real purchase. Compare that to spending $500 on a social media ad campaign and hoping it converts. Every dollar you spend on LetsGo is directly tied to revenue that already walked through your door."

### "We already use Yelp / Google Ads."

"Those are great for visibility, but you're paying for clicks and impressions regardless of whether someone actually visits. With LetsGo, you pay zero until a verified customer spends money at your business. And unlike Yelp, LetsGo builds loyalty — your customers earn more the more they visit, which means they choose you over your competitors because they've invested in leveling up at your spot."

### "What if someone submits a fake receipt?"

"Every receipt goes through your approval queue. You see the photo, the amount, and the date. You approve it, reject it, or dispute it. You have full control. You can also set auto-approval for receipts under a certain amount to save time on small tickets while manually reviewing larger ones. And our platform has built-in fraud detection systems to flag suspicious activity."

### "We tried Groupon and it didn't work."

"Groupon's model is fundamentally different from ours. Groupon attracts deal-seekers who come once for a deep discount and never return. LetsGo does the opposite — the reward starts small and gets bigger over time. A first-time visitor gets 5% back. By the time they've visited 20+ times, they're getting 10%. The incentive is to keep coming back, not to grab a deal and leave. We build regulars, not one-time bargain hunters."

### "10% platform fee sounds high."

"It's capped at $5 per receipt, so for any check over $50, the effective rate drops fast. A $100 dinner is only a 5% fee. A $200 dinner is only 2.5%. And remember — that fee only exists on the Basic plan, which has zero monthly costs. If your volume is high enough, Premium at $100/month with no per-receipt fees will save you money. ### "We don't have time to approve receipts."

"We've made it as easy as possible. You can set auto-approval for receipts under a dollar threshold you choose — say, $50. Everything under that gets approved automatically. For larger receipts, it's one tap to approve. Most business owners spend less than 5 minutes a week on receipt management. And you can designate a manager or staff member to handle it from their own login."

### "What's the catch? Why is the Basic plan free?"

"There's no catch. On the Basic plan, we make our money through the per-receipt platform fee — 10% of the subtotal, capped at $5. If a customer visits your business and you approve their receipt, we earn a small fee. If nobody visits, neither of us pays anything. Our incentive is exactly aligned with yours: drive real customers to your business."

### "How many users does LetsGo have?"

Be honest about where the platform is. If you're in early stages: "We're actively building our user base in this market, which means you have the opportunity to be one of the first businesses in the area. Early partners get the most visibility because there's less competition in the feed. As we onboard more businesses and run marketing campaigns, the user base grows — and your business is already established and collecting loyal customers."

### "Can I change my plan or payout tiers later?"

"Absolutely. You can switch between Basic and Premium at any time. If you go from Premium to Basic, the change takes effect at the end of your current billing period. You can also adjust your payout tiers whenever you want — changes apply to future visits only, never retroactively."
